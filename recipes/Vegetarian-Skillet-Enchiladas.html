Vegetarian Enchilada Skillet

Vegetarian Enchiladas

Easy to prepare and afford vegetarian option for dinner. Get the taste of the border in 25 minutes or less!

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp canola oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium sweet red pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can (10 oz) enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/8 tsp pepper
  • 8 corn tortillas (6 in), cut into 1/2 in strips
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • Optional: Chopped fresh cilantro, sliced avocado, sliced radishes, sour cream, and lime wedges

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°. Heat oil in a 10-in. cast-iron or other ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add onion and pepper; cook and stir until tender, 2-3 minutes. Add garlic; cook 1 minute longer.
  3. Stir in beans, enchilada sauce, corn, chili powder, cumin and pepper. Stir in tortilla strips.
  4. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat; simmer, uncovered, until tortilla strips are softened, 3-5 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle with cheese. Bake, uncovered, until sauce is bubbly and cheese is melted, 3-5 minutes.
  6. Garnish with optional ingredients as desired.
  7. For storage: These vegetarian skillet enchiladas last for about 2-3 days in the fridge. If you'd like to enjoy this vegetarian dinner for longer, you can freeze them in an air-tight container for up to 2-3 months.
